    • Statistics on Cardiac Arrest

  • Each year, more than 350,000 out-of-hospital cardiac arrests occur in the U.S., resulting in approximately 436,000 deaths.
  • Immediate CPR can double or triple a person's chance of survival after cardiac arrest.
  • The overall survival rate for out-of-hospital cardiac arrests is less than 10%
  • Without immediate CPR, the chance of survival from an out-of-hospital cardiac arrest decreases by 7-10% for every minute of delay
